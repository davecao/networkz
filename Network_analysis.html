3 ネットワークの構築

論文によく使うシンボルは、グラフG、頂点セットV、辺セットEとし、 あるグラフGが \(G=(V, E)\) で表す。

3.1 頂点間の距離

頂点Aと頂点Bがどのぐらい離れることによって 頂点Aと頂点Bの間に辺をつけます。いわゆる、 辺が頂点間の距離による定義します。 次は、よく使う距離の定義を紹介します。

3.2 都市ブロック距離 (City Block Distance)

\[d = \sum_{i=1}^{k} |a_{i} - b_{i}|\]

Note:　City Block distanceは \(d \ge 0\), マンハッタン(Manhattan distance)距離とも呼ばれます.

In most cases, this distance measure yields results similar to the Euclidean distance. Note, however, that with City block distance, the effect of a large difference in a single dimension is dampened (since the distances are not squared).

The name City block distance (also referred to as Manhattan distance) is explained if you consider two points in the xy-plane. The shortest distance between the two points is along the hypotenuse, which is the Euclidean distance. The City block distance is instead calculated as the distance in x plus the distance in y, which is similar to the way you move in a city (like Manhattan) where you have to move around the buildings instead of going straight through.

3.3 ユークリッド距離 (Euclidean Distance)

k次元の点aと点bの間のユークリッド距離は次の式で計算する。

\[d = \sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^{k} (a_{i} - b_{i})^2}\]

Note: ユークリッド距離は \(d \ge 0\)

The Euclidean distance between two points, a and b, with k dimensions is calculated as shown. The Euclidean distance is always greater than or equal to zero. The measurement would be zero for identical points and high for points that show little similarity.

3.4 平方ユークリッド距離 Squared Euclidean Distance

k次元の点aと点bの間の平方ユークリッド距離は次の式で計算する。

\[d = \sum_{i=1}^{k} (a_{i} - b_{i})^2\]

3.5 Half Squared Eculidean Distance

\[d = \frac{1}{2}\sum_{i=1}^{k} (a_{i} - b_{i})^2\]

3.6 Pearson’s相関係数(Correlation coefficient)

k次元の点Aと点Bの相関は、以下の計算式で計算する。 \[d = \frac{cov(A, B)}{\sigma_A\sigma_B} \]

Note: 相関係数は無次元量で、−1以上1以下の実数に値をとる。

3.7 Cosine Correlation

k次元の点Aと点Bの相関は、以下の計算式で計算する。 \[ d = \frac{A\cdot B}{||A||||B||} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{k}A_i B_i}{\sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^{k}A_{i}^{2}}\sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^{k}B_{i}^{2}}}\]

Note: Cosine Correlationの範囲が−１から+1まで (+１はaとbの類似度が一番高いと示す).

3.8 Tanimoto係数(Tanimoto Coefficient)

k次元の点Aと点Bの相関は、以下の計算式で計算する。 \[ T_s = \frac{\sum_{i}(A_i \bigwedge B_i)}{\sum_{i}(A_i \bigvee B_i)} \] \[d = -log_{2}(T_s)\] 相同性がnon-zeroの場合, ここでの\(\bigwedge\) と \(\bigvee\) はbitwiseのand, orの操作です。

Note: Tanimoto係数はJaccard係数の拡張バーションです。バイナリーの場合、Jaccard係数になって 範囲が0から+1まで (+１はaとbの類似度が一番高いと示す).

\[d = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{k}a_i\times b_i}{\sum_{i=1}^{k}a_{i}^2 + \sum_{i=1}^{k}b_{i}^2 - \sum_{i=1}^{k}a_i\times b_i}\]

3.9 Tanimoto係数(Jaccard係数の拡張バージョン)の例

A = [“リンゴ”,“ブドウ”,“イチゴ”,“パイン”,“キウイ”,“メロン”]

B = [“メロン”,“イチゴ”,“リンゴ”,“パインアップル”]

全部の種類で、“リンゴ”,“ブドウ”,“イチゴ”,“パイン”,“キウイ”,“メロン”,“パインアップル”を用いて、 AとBをベクトル化する。

\[ A = [1, 1, 1, 1, 1, 1, 0] \] \[ B = [1, 0, 1, 0, 0, 1, 1] \]

3.10 Tanimoto係数とJaccard係数の例 - Con’t

AとBの長さは、

\begin{aligned} \|A\|^{2} &= 1^{2} + 1^{2} + 1^{2} + 1^{2} + 1^{2} + 1^{2} + 0^{2} = 6 \\ \|B\|^{2} &= 1^{2} + 0^{2} + 1^{2} + 0^{2} + 0^{2} + 1^{2} + 1^{2} = 4 \end{aligned}

3.11 Tanimoto係数とJaccard係数の例 - Con’t

Tanimoto係数：　

\begin{aligned} T(A, B) &= \frac{A\cdot B}{\|A\|^{2} + \|B\|^{2} - A\cdot B} \\ &= \frac{3}{6+4-3} \\ &= \frac{3}{7} \end{aligned}

Jaccard係数: \(A \cap B = 3\) と \(A\cup B = 7\)

\[J = \frac{ A\cap B}{A\cup B } = \frac{3}{7} \]

4 ネットワークの統計 (undirected graph)

4.1 クラスタリング係数

クラスタリング係数は、グラフ内のノードが一緒にクラスタ化される傾向の程度の尺度です。 以下の2つを紹介します。

  1. Global clustering coefficient
  2. Local clustering coefficient

4.1.1 Local clustering coefficient

\[ C_i = \frac{2 m_i}{d_i (d_i - 1)} \] \(d_i\) はノード \(i\) のdegree, \(m_i\) はノード \(i\) の隣接ノード間のペアの数です。 左の図のように、ノード0のlocal clustering coefficientは、 \[ C_i = \frac{2\times 2}{4\times(4-1)} = \frac{1}{3} \] \(m_i\)(青い線の数)が2、\(d_i\) が4です。

\(d_i\) is the degree of node \(i\). \(m_i\) is the number of pairs among node \(i\)’s friends. The local clustering coefficient is 0 when the degree of nodes which is 1 since the denominator cannot be zero.

4.1.2 Global clustering coefficient

ネットワーク全体でのクラスタリングを測る。

  • 方法1. グラフ中に各ノードのローカルクラスタリング係数の平均値。　　
  • 方法2. Closed triplets(或は3x三角形)が全tripletsに対しての割合です。　　
\begin{aligned} CC &=\frac{\#\:of\:closed\:triplets}{\#\:of\:triplets} \\ &=\frac{3\times \#\:of\:triangles}{\#\:of\:triplets} \end{aligned}

Global clustering coefficient is the number of closed triplets (or 3 x triangles) over the total number of triplets (both open and closed).

4.2 平均ノード間距離

ネットワーク(グラフ) \(G\) 内の全てのノード間距離の平均値 \[\bar{d} = \sum_{u,v\in V}\frac{d(u, v)}{n(n-1)} \] \(d(u, v)\) は頂点セット \(V\) 内に頂点 \(u\) と頂点 \(v\) の間に最短距離です。 \(n\) はグラフ \(G\) の頂点の数です。　

左図の平均ノード間距離は、\(d = 4.6\) です。

4.3 ネットワーク半径(radius)、径(diameter)と離心数(eccentricity)

ネットワーク(グラフ) \(G(V, E)\) において, ある頂点 \(v\) の離心数 \(e_{G}(v)\) が \(G\) 内他の頂点と最大距離と定義する。

  • ネットワーク半径(radius): \[ rad(G) = \min_{v\in V}e_{G}(v) \]
  • ネットワーク径(diameter): \[ diam(G) = \max_{v\in V}e_{G}(v) \]
  • ネットワーク平均離心数(eccentricity): \[ \bar{ecc(G)} = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{v\in V}e_{G}(v)\]

The eccentricity eG(v) of a vertex v in G is the largest distance from v to another vertex of G, i.e. max{d(v, w)| w ∈ V (G)}. The diameter of G is the maximum eccentricity in G, denoted by diam(G).

6.1 Pearson相関係数（Pearson’s correlation coefficient）

ここで、 遺伝子ペアの発現の類似性（\(s_{ij}\)）は、

  1. 絶対値

    \[ s_{ij} = |corr(i, j)| \]

  2. 符号を維持するため

    \[ s_{ij} = \frac{1 + corr(i, j)}{2} \]

6.2 遺伝子ペア(辺)の選択: threshold

  1. signum関数

    \[a_{ij} = signum(s_{ij}, \tau) \equiv \begin{cases} 1 \quad \text{if}\,\, s_{ij} \ge \tau \\ 0 \quad \text{if}\,\, s_{ij} < \tau \end{cases}\]

  2. sigmoid関数

    \[a_{ij}=sigmoid(s_{ij}, \alpha, \tau_0)\equiv\frac{1}{1+e^{-\alpha(s_{ij}-\tau_0)}}\]

  3. Power関数

    \[a_{ij}=power(s_{ij}, \beta)\equiv|s_{ij}|^{\beta}\]

6.3 遺伝子ペア（辺）の選択: scale free criterion

赤：A exponentially truncated power law 黒：冪乗則

Scale-free topology \[ p(k) \sim k^{-\gamma} \]

  • 線形モデル \[ log_{10}(p(k)) \sim log_{10}(k) \]
  • A exponentially truncated power law \[ p(k)\sim k^{-\gamma}exp(-\alpha k) \]

Zhang, B., Horvath, S., 2005. A general framework for weighted gene co-expression network analysis. Stat Appl Genet Mol Biol 4, Article17. 10.2202/1544-6115.1128

6.4 Recommendation of scale free criterion

Only consider those parameter values that lead to a network satisfying scale- free topology at least approximately, e.g. signed \(R^2 > 0.80\).

Zhang, B., Horvath, S., 2005. A general framework for weighted gene co-expression network analysis. Stat Appl Genet Mol Biol 4, Article17. 10.2202/1544-6115.1128

6.5 遺伝子（頂点）の連結度(Connectivity)

  • 点連結度 \[k_i = \sum_{j=1}^{n} a_{ij} \]
  • Topological Overlap Matrix(TOM)-based \[w_i = \sum_{j=1}^{n} w_{ij}, \text{where}\quad w_{ij} = \frac{l_{ij}+a_{ij}}{min\{k_i,k_j\}+1-a_{ij}} \] \(l_{ij}=\sum_{u}a_{iu}a_{uj}\), and \(k_i=\sum_{u}a_{iu}\)

6.6 TOMの例

(＊)の数字は頂点のクラスタリング係数です。辺の数字はTOMの値です。

頂点Hと頂点Jの度数は \(k_{H} = 3\) と \(k_{J} = 3\) になり、 頂点Hと頂点Jと同時に連結の頂点の数は1となります。

\begin{eqnarray*} w(H,J) &=& \frac{l_{ij}+a_{ij}}{min\{k_i,k_j\}+1-a_{ij}} \\ &=&\frac{1+1}{3+1-1} \\ &=&\frac{2}{3} \end{eqnarray*}

7.6 Composite Index of Dynamical network biomarker(DNB)

  1. 有力なグループを有意性(student-t検定)で決定する
  2. Composite Indexを計算する

\[ I \stackrel{\mathrm{def}}{=} \frac{SD_d\cdot PCC_d}{PCC_o} \]

ここで、\(PCC_d\) は有力なグループなPearson相関係数の絶対値の平均値、\(PCC_o\) は 有力なグループと以外な分子なPearson相関係数の絶対値の平均値です。

\[ I \stackrel{\mathrm{def}}{=} \frac{SD_d\cdot PCC_d}{PCC_o} \] where \(PCC_d\) is the average Pearson’s correlation coefficient of the dominant group in absolute value; \(PCC_o\) is the average Pearson’s correlation coefficient of the dominant group with other molecules in absolute value; \(SD_d\) is the average standard deviation of the dominant group.

Gene GO Enrichment Analysis

“Enrichment” - ランダムと比べということ

To perform functional enrichment analysis, we need to have:

  • A set of genes of interest (e.g., differentially expressed genes): study set (n)
  • A set with all the genes to consider in the analysis: population set (N)

(which must contain the study set)

  • GO annotations, associating the genes in the population set to GO terms
  • The GO ontology, with the description of GO terms and their relationships

For each GO term, we need to count the frequency (k) of genes in the study set (n) that are associated to the term, and the frequency (K) of genes in the population set (N) that are associated to the same term. Then we test how likely would it be to obtain at least k genes associated to the term if n genes would be randomly sampled from the population, given the frequency K and size N of the population.

The appropriate statistical test is the one-tailed variant of Fisher’s exact test, also known as the hypergeometric test for over-representation. When the one-tailed version is applied, this test will compute the probability of observing at least the sample frequency, given the population frequency. The hypergeometric distribution measures precisely the probability of k successes in n draws, without replacement, from a finite population of size N that contains exactly K successful objects:

\[ P(X=k) = \frac{C_{K}^{k} C_{N-n}^{K-k}}{C_{N}^{n}} \]

Gene Set Enrichment Analysis and pathway analysis

  • Gene Set Enrichment Analysis (GSEA, DAVID)

    Subramanian et al.Gene set enrichment analysis: A knowledge-based approach for interpreting genome-wide expression profiles, PNAS 102(43)15545-15550, 2005

  • Pathway analysis (KEGG, Reactome, Ingenuity)

    Curtis, R.K., et al. (2005) Pathways to the analysis of microarray data. Trends in Biotechnology 23: 429-435. Werner, T. (2008) Bioinformatics applications for pathway analysis of microarray data. Current Opinion in Biotechnology 19: 50-54.

The Gene Ontology, containing standardised annotation of gene products, is commonly used for this purpose. It works by comparing the frequency of individual annotations in the gene list (e.g differentially expressed genes) with a reference list (usually all genes on the microarray or in the genome). Enrichment of biological pathways supplied by KEGG, Ingenuity, Reactome or WikiPathways can be performed in a similar way.

A common approach to interpreting gene expression data is gene set enrichment analysis based on the functional annotation of the differentially expressed genes (Figure 13). This is useful for finding out if the differentially expressed genes are associated with a certain biological process or molecular function.
